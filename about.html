About Rentaca

Every Ithaca rental, honestly aggregated.

Rentaca pulls public rental listings from across Ithaca into one searchable, building-centric map — so you can compare the whole market without 15 browser tabs.

Why building-first?

Most listing sites show you a flat feed of units. Review sites show you buildings but no live availability. Rentaca links them: every unit is grouped under its building, so you can see everything available in a place alongside its photos, floorplans, resident reviews, and walk time to campus — in one view.

How pricing is shown

We display the monthly rent exactly as listed at the source, plus a per-person figure (total rent ÷ bedrooms) so a 4-bedroom house and a studio can be compared fairly. Where a source only publishes a building-level or starting price, the number reflects that — always confirm the exact rent with the landlord.

Rents and availability in a college town move weekly. Treat everything here as a strong starting point, not a contract. Click through to the original listing or the landlord's site to verify.

Where the data comes from

Listings are aggregated from public sources across the web, including Craigslist, AppFolio-powered property managers, local landlord sites, and the Cornell-affiliated CUAPTS review database. Reviews and star ratings come from CUAPTS; photos and floorplans come from the original listings.

Trust & limitations

  • Not all buildings have photos, pricing, or reviews — coverage varies by source.
  • Image links point to the original hosts and can occasionally expire.
  • Rentaca is an independent project, not affiliated with Cornell University or Ithaca College.
  • This is an aggregator that links back to public sources — it does not handle applications or payments.
